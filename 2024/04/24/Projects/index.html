2024.6- Understanding the possible light-quark spin symmetry from the perspective of large-Nc limit

2024.1-2024.8 Solving two-nucleon system in a boosted box

This work is a part of and to support the program of harmonic-oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET). One goal of HOBET is to determine the low-energy constants of the effective theory directly from lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) calculation of two-nucleon spectrum in a boosted finite volume. Here we mimic the results of the LQCD calculation with a known spin-dependent potential, extracting physical observables including the energy spectrum and the angular momentum contents of the resulting eigenstates. The approach employed is solving the Schrödinger equation on a boosted (thus bent and non-orthogonal) lattice. Moreover, we use this example to understand the impact of periodicity and finite volume on the potential and the spectrum.

2023.10-2024.4 Investigating entanglement in the low-energy sector of heavy mesons

This work explores a conjecture known as entanglement suppression which is proposed as one possible origin of emergent symmetries, in the context of heavy meson scatterings. We find that entanglement suppression indeed leads to the enlargement of the inherent heavy-quark spin symmetry of the heavy meson system, SU(2)×SU(2), to a light-quark spin symmetry, SU(4), and predicts additional siblings for X(3872) and Tcc(3875)+. See our paper here: Tao-Ran Hu, Su Chen, and Feng-Kun Guo, Entanglement suppression and low-energy scattering of heavy mesons, arXiv:2404.05958 [hep-ph].

2023.3-2023.12 Conducting a complete calculation for pion axioproduction process

This work incorporates the axion into the framework of chiral perturbation theory and investigates the pion axioproduction process aN → πN. We perform the first calculation of the contribution of the Roper resonance to this process, obtaining the complete partial-wave cross sections (experimental observables) with significant contributions from resonance states. The pions produced by intermediate resonance states in this process may be observable by Cherenkov detectors, thereby assisting us in capturing axion signals from supernovae. See our paper here: Cheng-Cheng Li, Tao-Ran Hu, Feng-Kun Guo, and Ulf-G. Meißner, Pion axioproduction revisited, Phys. Rev. D 109, 075050 (2024), arXiv:2312.02564 [hep-ph].
