2023.10-2024.4 Investigating entanglement in the low-energy sector of heavy mesons
This work explores a conjecture known as entanglement suppression which is proposed as one possible origin of emergent symmetries, in the context of heavy meson scatterings. We find that entanglement suppression indeed leads to the enlargement of the inherent heavy-quark spin symmetry of the heavy meson system, SU(2)×SU(2), to a light-quark spin symmetry, SU(4), and predicts additional siblings for X(3872) and Tcc(3875)+. See our paper here: Tao-Ran Hu, Su Chen, and Feng-Kun Guo, Entanglement suppression and low-energy scattering of heavy mesons, arXiv:2404.05958 [hep-ph].
2023.3-2023.12 Conducting a complete calculation for pion axioproduction process
This work incorporates the axion into the framework of chiral perturbation theory and investigates the pion axioproduction process aN → πN. We perform the first calculation of the contribution of the Roper resonance to this process, obtaining the complete partial-wave cross sections with significant contributions from resonance states (experimental observables). The pions produced by intermediate resonance states in this process may be observable by Cherenkov detectors, thereby assisting us in capturing axion signals from supernovae. See our paper here: Cheng-Cheng Li, Tao-Ran Hu, Feng-Kun Guo, and Ulf-G. Meißner, Pion axioproduction revisited, arXiv:2312.02564 [hep-ph].
